EFFECTIVENESS OF INTERPERSONAL SKILLS
Interpersonal skills are important for good communication and strong teamwork in many areas of life, from the workplace to event planning. These skills are used daily to work effectively with others, and without them, a team is unlikely to achieve its objectives or perform successfully. This section explores the interpersonal skills demonstrated during both the design and delivery stages of the Black Hole Exploration event proposal. The key interpersonal skills applied throughout the project included verbal and non-verbal communication, listening, negotiation, problem-solving, decision-making, and assertiveness. Each of these skills contributes to effective collaboration, and most were demonstrated consistently during the project.
Design Stage
During the design stage of the proposal, effective communication was essential to ensure that both team members were comfortable and satisfied with the direction of the event. This was initially demonstrated through meetings where ideas for the Black Hole Exploration conference were proposed and discussed. Both my teammate and I shared different concepts, and while the original ideas differed, strong listening and negotiation skills were demonstrated when redeveloping or updating the proposal based on key factors such as location, venue capacity, audience suitability, staffing, guest speakers and feasibility.
As the proposal developed, communication became more structured and focused. Clear verbal discussion and mutual understanding allowed us to research and finalise decisions efficiently, resulting in little to no problems or delay. This demonstrated effective teamwork and problem-solving, as compromises were made where necessary to strengthen the overall quality of the proposal.
Communication during the design stage was not limited to face-to-face meetings. Due to limited time for our personal lives and external commitments, online communication and virtual meetings were also used. These sessions allowed us to brainstorm ideas, share research findings, and make collaborative decisions remotely. This required written communication skills, clarity in messaging, and responsibility in meeting self-imposed deadlines. Despite being less formal, these methods were effective in keeping the project progressing and ensuring both team members remained engaged.
Overall, interpersonal skills during the design stage were used effectively. Respectful communication, active listening, and clear contributions ensured that tasks were completed on time and to a high standard. While online communication occasionally went off-topic, this did not significantly impact productivity, and the design stage was completed successfully.
Delivery Stage
The delivery stage followed shortly after the completion of the design phase and initially caused some pressure within the team due to time constraints. On the day of the event, minor communication issues occurred regarding meeting locations, largely due to last-minute changes and nerves. This highlighted how stress can temporarily affect communication clarity.
However, once the session began, communication improved significantly. The presentation was delivered clearly and confidently, with effective verbal communication and appropriate non-verbal cues such as eye contact and body language. Although the session was initially slightly one-sided, enthusiasm and confidence quickly engaged the audience, demonstrating strong interpersonal communication skills.
During the session, problem-solving and decision-making were required when managing audience engagement and maintaining appropriate noise levels. The team responded calmly and effectively, regaining attention while ensuring the event continued as planned. This demonstrated strong teamwork, responsibility, and adaptability. The session concluded on time, with opportunities for discussion and reflection, showing that the original plan was successfully followed.
Evaluation of Performance
Team Dynamics and Roles
Within the team, I naturally adopted a leadership and organisational role early in the project, largely because the initial event concept originated from my ideas. This helped streamline decision-making in the early stages and strengthened communication by providing direction. However, decisions were still made collaboratively, and feedback from my teammate was consistently encouraged and implemented.
Using Belbin’s Team Role theory, I identified my role as a Resource Investigator, which aligns with my enthusiasm for generating ideas, seeking opportunities, and contributing creatively to the project. While I agreed with many aspects of this role, I did not fully identify with the suggested weakness of losing interest after initial excitement. Overall, the Belbin model supported my contribution to the project, particularly in problem-solving and adaptability.
Work Outlook and Contribution
The workload was divided fairly between my teammate and I with our responsibilities clearly understood. Although motivation occasionally fluctuated due to external commitments, open communication allowed these issues to be addressed quickly. The two-man team size helped maintain accountability and ensured all tasks were completed.
We both contributed strongly to the organisation, communication, and delivery, particularly when engaging with the audience. However, reflecting on my performance, allowing another team member to take a more prominent leadership role definitely helped to further strengthen the project. This reflection demonstrates personal development and a growing understanding of effective teamwork.
Conclusion
In conclusion, interpersonal skills were used effectively throughout both the design and delivery stages of the event. Strong communication, teamwork, and problem-solving contributed to the success of the project. While online communication could be improved in future projects, the overall objectives of the event were achieved, and the session was well received by attendees. This experience has increased confidence in applying interpersonal skills and highlighted areas for further development in future event planning scenarios.
